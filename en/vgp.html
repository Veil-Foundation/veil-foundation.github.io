The Veil Foundation seeks to provide grants to organizations and individuals aiming to improve the Veil Project, and its mission to create the world's best privacy cryptocurrency.

Veil Grant Proposal Process

The process for submitting a proposal is as follows:

  1. Scope — The scope of activities subject to funding is broad, and can include technical improvements to the Veil protocol itself, development of consumer facing products, and/or marketing and strategic partnership activities that promote adoption of the currency. Improvements to the Veil protocol itself should be accompanied by a Veil Improvement Protocol (VIP), submitted to and approved by the Veil development team.
  2. Budget — The maximum grant amount is $10,000, paid in Veil, and limited to a maximum of 10,000 Veil (if the value of Veil at the time is less than $1.00.)
  3. Template — The proposal should follow the template provided below.
  4. Delivery — The proposal should be provided electronically in the form of a PDF document, sent to grants@veil-foundation.org. If the size of the PDF is greater than 25MB, please send a link to download the document using a file-sharing service.
  5. Receipt Confirmation — The Veil Foundation will respond with a confirmation of receipt within 24-48 hours.
  6. Evaluation & Response — The evaluation period will generally take less than four weeks, during which time the Foundation may followup with additional informational queries. The Foundation will provide a response, either accepting the proposal for funding, or declining it.
  7. Funding — Successful proposals may receive immediate funding, or may be required to wait for the next Veil Superblock budget emission, which generally happens during the first week of each month. Prior to provision of funding, the grant will be formalized in a contract signed between the Foundation and recipient.
  8. Monitoring — Upon funding and kick-off of the activity, grant recipients are required to provide progress reports by the first day of each month.

Veil Grant Proposal Template

Executive Summary

Introduce your organization (or yourself if an individual), state its mission, summarize the project being proposed and how the project you are looking to get funded fits your mission.

Statement of Need

Present a clear, brief but concrete definition of the problem you are seeking to solve.

Goals and Objectives

Describe your envisioned outcome, should the proposal get funded. This is a description of the consequences of solving the problem, beginning with a general overview and drilling down to specifics that are measurable.

Methods and Strategies

This is a description of "how" you will solve the problem, bridging the problem you've stated with the goals and objectives of solving that problem. This is the most important aspect of the proposal, as it's the specific activity you are asking to get funded.

Plan of Evaluation

Describe how you will evaluate your project on an ongoing basis, to ensure the funds are being used efficiently and to the ends justifying the grant.

Budget

In this section, provide a detailed breakdown of how the grant will be used, and a mapping of staged funding payments to milestones.

Organization Information

Provide a history of the organization, or yourself if an individual, including detailed biographies of key organizational members. Include registered address, and contact details.
